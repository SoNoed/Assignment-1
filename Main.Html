The Whale Shark is a slow-moving carpet shark and is the largest known extant fish species.
They are currently classified as Endangered on the Conservation status.
The whale shark inhabits all tropical and warm-temperate seas.
It is primarily pelagic, and can be found in both coastal and oceanic habitats.

Shark Habitat

Whale sharks possess a broad, flattened head with a large mouth and two small eyes located at the front corners.Unlike many other sharks, whale shark mouths are located at the front of the head rather than on the underside of the head.
Their mouths can contain over 300 rows of tiny teeth and 20 filter pads which it uses to filter feed.
The spiracles are located just behind the eyes. Whale sharks have five large pairs of gills.
Their skin is dark grey with a white belly marked with an arrangement of pale grey or white spots and stripes that is unique to each individual.
